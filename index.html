5 steps to get ready
1. A few days before the session avoid alcohol and blood thinners.
2. Eat well before the session.
3. Wear or bring comfortable clothes.
4. Dry skin requires additional moisturizing during a week before the session.
5. Cover from sun the area of the skin where tattoo will be done.
Aftercare Routine
I see no reason to pay expensive for a tattoo if you don’t plan take care of it, it would be very stupid.

So if you want to keep your quality expensive accessory in good condition for a long time, make sure you follow these recommendations:
- Wash the tattoo with a warm water and baby soap or a special aftercare product.
- Dry with a paper towel and apply a healing cream 3-4 times a day.
(I recommend a special for healing tattoos cream, but you can use any moisturizing one).
- Cover the tattoo with a disposable baby diaper when going out, please make sure it doesn’t sweat under the diaper.
- Avoid rubbing, scratching, tearing off crusts.
- Skip swimming, sauna and physical activity for 2 weeks.
- In sunny period use the sunscreen/ SPF and avoid direct sun rays.

Additional recommendations may be added with skin with specifics.
General principles of Tattoo aftercare
Everybody wants a quality tattoo, but only a few are ready to aftercare properly of a fresh tattoo.
If you don’t aftercare a fresh tattoo, it doesn’t matter what a brilliant master did it for you, the result will be the only one - a shitty tattoo.

First, aftercare is a mandatory step to make the healing process go smoothly and timely. Aftercare also let a tattoo stay clean, bright and contrast longer.
Second, the first days of aftercare are the most critical as the human’s body perceives a tattoo as a foreign body and tries to get rid of it in every possible way. The immune and lymphatic systems start working harder, you can experience higher temperature and weakness. So please mind that and be careful and cautious. Allow yourself being in a comfort and stay healthy. As a healthy body is much faster to heal your tattoo.
Third, colored tattoos need a little bit more attention and aftercare than Black&grey, as they are painted more densely, so the skin is more traumatized then. Please also mind the state of your skin and choose individual aftercare with your master.

Stages of work in Tattoo
What happens after you have contacted me:
1. Consultation ( online or offline)
2. We agree on the day of appointment and I prepare the sketch.
3. You come to the salon and we decide on location and size of the tattoo.
4. After that I start to prepare the workplace and transfer for copy on the body. It takes around 20-25 minutes, during this time you can drink/eat something.
5. We do a tattoo (In next post I will tell you about a tattoo-session duration).
6. Upon completion I treat the skin and give you recommendations for the aftercare of the tattoo.
7. After 1.5 - 2 months you come to me to check if the correction is needed.
